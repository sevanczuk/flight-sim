## Amendment Summary (V1 → V2)

V2 reflects the following changes from V1 per D-12:

1. **Primary instrument pivoted** from GNC 355 to GNX 375. The real-world motivation was always to replicate the avionics in the two C172s Steve flies; the more-frequently-flown aircraft has the GNX 375. Original "GNC 355" scoping was traced to a nomenclature confusion ("GNC" vs. "GNX").
2. **Scope expanded** to include Mode S transponder operation (pp. 75–85 of Pilot's Guide), built-in dual-link ADS-B In/Out as a primary concern (not external-dependent as in 355 case), and full procedural fidelity for instrument approaches.
3. **Stream C restructured** per D-11 (outline-first) and D-12 (Option 5: harvest 355 outline → dedicated 375 outline). Sub-tasking introduced: C2.1 (outline), C2.2 (spec body). C2.2 format deferred per D-13 pending 375 outline review.
4. **Status updates** for Streams A, B, and C (C1 complete). The 355 work product (`docs/specs/GNC355_Functional_Spec_V1_outline.md`) is shelved pending 355 implementation, not deleted.

Full pivot rationale including all 5 options considered: `docs/specs/pivot_355_to_375_rationale.md`.

## 1. Purpose

Coordinate the preparatory workstreams that must complete before actual GNX 375 Air Manager plugin implementation begins. Each stream produces concrete deliverables; the three converge into a position where a GNX 375 design spec can be written with confidence.

**Estimated duration:** 30–60 min CC wall-clock.

The 355 outline estimated 2,800 lines and recommended piecewise+manifest; the 375 outline may differ (XPDR adds ~100 lines; COM removal subtracts ~200 lines; net roughly comparable but structure differs).

**Estimated duration:** 4–8 hours CC wall-clock total across the selected format's sub-tasks; CD drafting overhead ~2 hours.

## 9. Risks and Mitigations (V2)

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|--------|------------|
| GNX 375 XPDR dataref/event names not readily documented in XPL or MSFS ecosystems | High | Medium | C2.1-375 outline flags these as research gaps; spec may leave placeholders to be filled during design phase. Garmin's official XPL/MSFS avionics mod documentation is the primary reference. |
