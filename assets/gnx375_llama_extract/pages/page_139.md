# HOT SPOTS

![Map view showing a SafeTaxi hot spot circled in magenta on an airport diagram.](page_139_image_1_v2.jpg)

SafeTaxi hot spots identify locations on an airport surface where positional confusion or runway incursions are likely to occur. These known problem areas require heightened attention by pilots.

![Map interface showing a selected hot spot with a "TAXIWAY HOLD POSITION" label and a "Hot Spot Info" sidebar.](page_139_image_3_v2.jpg)

Selecting the border of a hot spot displays a brief summary of the indicated hazard and an information key.

![TAXIWAY HOLD POSITION label](page_139_image_6_v2.jpg)

![Hot Spot Info button for HOT SPOT 2](page_139_image_5_v2.jpg)

Tapping this key provides additional location information. Numbering corresponds to a list on the airport diagram.

![Detailed information box for HOT SPOT 2: HOLD LINE FOR RWY 03/21 IS ON TWY K. PILOTS SHOULD BE PREPARED TO HOLD SHORT OF RWY 21 ON TWY K UNLESS AN AUTHORIZATION TO CROSS HAS BEEN ISSUED BY ATC.](page_139_image_4_v2.jpg)

The following airport features may be deemed hot spots by aviation authorities.

* Intersecting taxiways and runways

* Complex ramp areas

* Directional limitations

* Limited wing-tip clearance

* Overflight risk
